About Me

I'm a senior at the University of Michigan studying Data Science and Mathematics. I'm passionate about computer graphics, systems engineering, robotics, and linear algebra.

On campus, I'm a member of the Autonomous Robotics Vehicle Team, where I focus on path planning algorithms and autonomous navigation. I’m also involved with Michigan Computer Graphics, where I explore topics ranging from ray marching to rasterization.

In my free time, I’m building a voxel engine from scratch using Rust and wgpu, combining my interests in graphics and systems programming. I also study Japanese language and culture, and had the opportunity to study abroad in Japan during the summer of 2024. Language learning has been a rewarding challenge that helps me approach problem-solving from new perspectives.

Projects

  • Voxel Engine: Built a real-time voxel engine using Rust and WGPU, featuring GPU-accelerated rendering and efficient chunk-based world management. Focused on performance and parallelism to handle dynamic terrain and lighting in a fully interactive 3D environment.

  • Ray Tracer: Built a high-fidelity ray tracer in Rust using object-oriented design and advanced linear algebra to simulate realistic graphics features like anti-aliasing, reflections, and refraction across various materials.

  • Sharded Paxos Database: Developed a fault-tolerant Paxos-based consensus library in Go, using idiomatic Go techniques such as goroutines and channels for concurrency. Integrated with a sharded key-value store to maintain reliability across simulated machine and replica group failures.
